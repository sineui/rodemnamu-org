Model Churches

Churches that inspire Rodemnamu Church

Rodemnamu Church aspires to learn from and emulate two churches found in Scripture and history.

These two model churches serve as a compass, showing us the direction we must pursue in an ever-changing world and modern society.

Antioch Church

The church at Antioch, recorded in Acts, stands as the defining example of missionary dynamism in the early church.

Sensitivity to the Work of the Holy Spirit

Born through ordinary, unnamed believers who obeyed the Spirit's leading and proclaimed the Lord Jesus

Raising Up Good People

Growth fueled by godly, Spirit-filled, and faithful leaders like Barnabas and Saul (Paul)

Teaching and Nurturing

The large crowd gathered by the Gospel was taught and nurtured to become disciples full of Christ

Generous Sharing

Their growth and maturity expressed itself in generous and liberal giving to others in need

Missionary Sending and Care

Commissioned and sent Barnabas and Paul, and continued to support and care for their mission work

Geneva Church

The church in Geneva served by 16th-century Reformer John Calvin was a community built on the foundation of Reformed theology, practicing a faith where Word and life were integrated.

Worship Balancing Word and Sacrament

Experiencing the presence of God through worship where preaching and the Lord's Supper were held in balance

Doctrinal Education Rooted in Reformed Theology

Systematically teaching and training the congregation through the Geneva Catechism

Faith Formation through Home and Church Partnership

A structure where families and the church together nurtured the next generation in the faith

Cultivating the Next Generation

Training and sending future leaders through the Geneva Academy

Social Commitment in the Spirit of Diakonia

Practicing the social responsibility of the Gospel through service to refugees and the poor

International Reformed Church Network

Spreading the Reformation movement through solidarity with Reformed churches across Europe
